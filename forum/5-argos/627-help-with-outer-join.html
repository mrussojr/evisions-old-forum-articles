So... I don't use PowerCampus or MSSQL (Banner/Oracle here) and I never use RIGHT JOINS, but near as I can figure I'd pick a different table as my main table depending on which students I was trying to get -- all those that appear in PEOPLEORGBALANCE that are also enrolled or all that appear in ACADEMIC regardless of whether they appear in PEOPLEORGBALANCE (with NULLs for the balance amount if they don't appear in PEOPLEORGBALANCE).  I'd possibly end up with one of these:

 

I'd join it something like this if I wanted everyone that had a cum/alltermcum balance amount and that was also an enrolled student


FROM PEOPLEORGBALANCE
JOIN PEOPLE ON PEOPLE.PEOPLE_CODE_ID = PEOPLEORGBALANCE.PEOPLE_ORG_CODE_ID
JOIN ACADEMIC ON ACADEMIC.PEOPLE_CODE_ID = PEOPLEORGBALANCE.PEOPLE_ORG_CODE_ID
             AND ACADEMIC.ACADEMIC_YEAR = PEOPLEORGBALANCE.ACADEMIC_YEAR 
             AND ACADEMIC.ACADEMIC_TERM = PEOPLEORGBALANCE.ACADEMIC_TERM 
             AND ACADEMIC.ACADEMIC_SESSION = :main_lb_yts.AcademicSession
             AND ACADEMIC.PRIMARY_FLAG ='Y'
             AND ACADEMIC.ENROLL_SEPARATION ='ENRL'
LEFT OUTER JOIN CHARGECREDIT ON CHARGECREDIT.PEOPLE_ORG_CODE_ID = PEOPLEORGBALANCE.PEOPLE_ORG_CODE_ID
                            AND CHARGECREDIT.ACADEMIC_YEAR = PEOPLEORGBALANCE.ACADEMIC_YEAR 
                            AND CHARGECREDIT.ACADEMIC_TERM = PEOPLEORGBALANCE.ACADEMIC_TERM 
                            AND CHARGECREDIT.CHARGE_CREDIT_TYPE <> 'C'
                            AND CHARGECREDIT.VOID_FLAG ='N'
                            AND CHARGECREDIT.ACADEMIC_SESSION = ACADEMIC.ACADEMIC_SESSION
WHERE PEOPLEORGBALANCE.ACADEMIC_YEAR = :main_lb_yts.AcademicYear
  AND PEOPLEORGBALANCE.ACADEMIC_TERM = :main_lb_yts.AcademicTerm
  AND PEOPLEORGBALANCE.SUMMARY_TYPE ='cum'
  AND PEOPLEORGBALANCE.BALANCE_TYPE ='alltermcum'

It may have to do with the fact that some of the things I had on the JOIN lines, you moved down to the WHERE. It can have a different effect there. It seems like it ought to be on the JOIN because you don't want to join in the records with a Credit_type=C, do you?    I'm not sure about MSSQL, but NULLs can be weird in that they don't equal anything but also don't not-equal anything. If you specifically want to keep NULLs, you might have to re-write it as -- and (CHARGECREDIT.CHARGE_CREDIT_TYPE <>'C' OR CHARGECREDIT.CHARGE_CREDIT_TYPE IS NULL )
