Good question, this one is a little tricky.  We have to bend the rules a bit in order to get second or third pages

 

First, in order to get more space to work with, you will (temporarily) set the report dimensions -- edit the properties of the report by right-clicking somewhere in the margins -- to a custom length.  I usually find that 8.5 x 22 gives me a total of two pages to work with.

 

Adjust your bands on the "first" page (e.g., first 11 inches) such that they are static.  What I've done is create a child band after all of the bands that need to fall on the first page and set its properties to "at bottom of page" and "force new page after".  So anything after that last band is going to start on a new page regardless of the size of your bands above it.  You can also set your other bands to a static height so they don't shrink or grow.  You would adjust your band heights and take into account any margins or page footers such that they are no more than 11 inches.

 

Once you have your first page assembled, you can move to the next 11 inches of your report.  Here you will assemble your second page.  

 

Now you can go back to the report properties and set the dimensions to 8.5 x 11 inches.  You can't see the second page, but it is there.  When you need to edit the report, go into the report dimensions again, set them to 8.5 x 22, make your adjustments, and again put the dimensions back to 8.5 x 11.

You can do it either way.  The most straightforward way is to use the custom length and add your charts in the space provided... and in the case of charts, size is pretty static so you can be pretty confident regarding which page they fall on.  For text or other types of variables, you may need to control what falls where, in which case I like to use the child band after, say, dynamic rich text fields, so that my page will break when I want it to.

 

As an example of this, one of our bursted financial aid letters has a list on the first page of outstanding items.  This list is dynamic based upon the individual student.  I know the lists are not long enough to break to the next page, but a few of the lists might be short enough to allow the table of costs featured at the top of page 2 to begin prematurely at the bottom of page one.  To prevent this, I insert a child band between the rich text field containing the list and the band containing the cost table so that I can set this middle child band to "Force new page after" -- which will ensure the cost table begins at the top of page two.  WHEW!  Hope that made more sense to you than to me
