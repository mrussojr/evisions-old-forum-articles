This is what I use.  The user prompt is for the aid year.  The terms are hidden from the user but available for the code.

Our scheme is 10  = fall, 20 = spring and 30 = summer.  We have a complication that summer can be either leading or trailing,  we need both for reports.

 

SELECT  ROBINST_AIDY_CODE as aidyr,
        summer2.stvterm_code as summer_lead_term,
        fall.stvterm_code as fall_term,
        spring.stvterm_code as spring_term,
        summer.stvterm_code as summer_trail_term
FROM   robinst,
       stvterm fall,
       stvterm spring,
       stvterm summer,
       stvterm summer2
where fall.stvterm_fa_proc_yr = robinst_aidy_code
  and substr(fall.stvterm_code,5,2) = '10'
  --
  and spring.stvterm_fa_proc_yr = robinst_aidy_code
  and substr(spring.stvterm_code,5,2) = '20'
  --
  and summer.stvterm_fa_proc_yr = robinst_aidy_code
  and substr(summer.stvterm_code,5,2) = '30'
  --
  and summer2.stvterm_fa_proc_yr = robinst_aidy_code - 101
  and substr(summer2.stvterm_code,5,2) = '30'
ORDER BY ROBINST_AIDY_CODE desc

If you just need a list of the term codes you could use listagg to get the list of term codes.  Something like this:

 

select listagg(stvterm_code, ', ') within group (order by stvterm_code)
from   stvterm
where (stvterm_desc like '%Fall%'
       or stvterm_desc like '%Spring%')
and   STVTERM_FA_PROC_YR = '1920';
