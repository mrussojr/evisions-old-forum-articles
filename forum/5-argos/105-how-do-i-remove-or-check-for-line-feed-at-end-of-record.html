I am using the SSBDESC table in a datablock that produces an output CSV file.  The problem I am encountering is that the field SSBDESC_TEXT_NARRATIVE, which is in a HUGECLOB format, has records that contain line feeds instead of carriage returns, causing part of the same line to be output onto a separate line in Excel.

 

Does anyone know of a way to "skip" these line feed cahractes within a HUGECLOB formatted line?

 

Thank you

A nested replace worked for me...

  • REPLACE(REPLACE(ssbdesc.ssbdesc_text_narrative, chr(10), ''), chr(13), '') as "CLEANED_TEXT_NARRATIVE_A"

 

If you also want to remove TABS, this works...

  • REPLACE(REPLACE(REPLACE(ssbdesc.ssbdesc_text_narrative, chr(10), ''), chr(13), ''), chr(9), '') as "CLEANED_TEXT_NARRATIVE_B"

 

REGEXP_REPLACE is also an option, but it might be at a higher cost...

  • regexp_replace(ssbdesc.ssbdesc_text_narrative, '['||chr(10)||chr(13)||chr(9)||']') as "CLEANED_TEXT_NARRATIVE_C"
