There is another table/form SCBCRKY / SCABASE that gives the base information for the course.  That is where the course is originally established with a beginning term.  Some schools use it more than others when a course is discontinued.  If the course is just changing names, credits etc.  a new record is created in the SCBCRSE table. If a course is completely retired and will NEVER be offered again, your school may go back into SCABASE and put an end term on the course.  In that case the records you show above would be getting that as the end term rather than the generic 999999 end of time.

 

Sorry I didn't mention it earlier, it just isn't used very often.  If that isn't your situation, then I have no clue ?

 

If the 82 at the beginning means 1982, the data could have been loaded from a prior SIS bypassing the normal Banner processes I suppose.

David, Another suggestion for finding where data comes from in Banner....  If I can't get info on a field from Banner's Dynamic Help, another place I check are Ellucian's Object Access Reporting Guides (downloadable from Ellucian). They show where the various Banner views (like Student Enrollment Summary) get their data from. Since Dynamic Help doesn't work for calculated values, the Object Access Guides can sometimes be used to tell if there's a function being used as a data source.
