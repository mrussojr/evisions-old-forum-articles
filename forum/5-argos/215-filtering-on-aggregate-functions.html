Ed,

 

I don't generally use the visual designer but I think this cryptic message suggested you are attempting to call an aggregate function in the where clause (that isn't a having?). Can you relocate the calculation in a subquery and the refer to the aliased field in your where clause? I found this in the help files: http://webhelp.evisions.com/HelpFiles/Argos/4.3/en/Default.htm#DataBlock Designer Guide/Summing and Grouping.htm%3FTocPath%3DDataBlock%20Designer%20Guide%20(Tutorials)|Advanced%20Query%20Techniques|_____1

 

I generally use the "free type" as it is more flexible. If you do that, than you can put a special tag in the where clause to activate additional filtering for each report

 

where....

--$addfilter

order by....

 

Then on each report, on the filters tab, check activate addition filtering and list your criteria (as a side note, the first line of your criteria does not need the AND keyword, but subsequent lines will)
