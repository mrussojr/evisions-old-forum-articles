I have received a request to show students who have applied but are not admitted, and also, in addition, those students who did get admitted but have not yet registered.

 

For those applied and not admitted, I am thinking of using the Banner form SAAADMS and checking the applicaiton status - if it is equal to "C", "I" or "W" then the student has applied but is not yet admitted.  Is there anything else I need to check for? 

 

For those who have been admitted but have not yet registered, I think the Banner form SZAREGS should be used and check for a status of "D", and should also use the table SFRSTCR, which if empty, would indicate the student has not yet registered.  And again, is there anything else I should check for?

 

If anyone has a datablock or two that handles things like this, I would love to see them.

Hi David!

 

 To make a list of students who have applied but not yet been admitted, we use something along the lines of: 

SELECT SPRIDEN_PIDM
  FROM SPRIDEN
 WHERE SPRIDEN_CHANGE_IND is NULL
   AND SPRIDEN_PIDM IN (SELECT SARADAP_PIDM
                          FROM SARADAP
                         WHERE SARADAP_TERM_CODE_ENTRY = :TermCode
                           AND SARADAP_PIDM in (SELECT SARAPPD_PIDM
                                                  FROM SARAPPD
                                                 WHERE SARAPPD_APDC_CODE = 'R'
                                                   AND SARAPPD_TERM_CODE_ENTRY = SARADAP_TERM_CODE_ENTRY)
                           AND SARADAP_PIDM not in (SELECT SARAPPD_PIDM
                                                      FROM SARAPPD
                                                     WHERE SARAPPD_APDC_CODE in('A','SA','CA','C','D')
                                                       AND SARAPPD_TERM_CODE_ENTRY = SARADAP_TERM_CODE_ENTRY))

 

Where the STVAPDC descsriptions for 'R' are 'Received' and the 'A','SA', and 'CA' are admit codes, the 'C' is cancelled, and the 'D' is deny.  These tables can be seen in Banner on the SAAADMS and SAADCRV screens.

 

This works for us, since we really only allow one App per term - you may need to tweak it so that the SARAPPD subqueries use SARAPPD_APPL_NO if you allow multiple apps per term code.

 

For the admitted not Enrolled, our code is like this:

  

SELECT SPRIDEN_PIDM
  FROM SPRIDEN
 WHERE SPRIDEN_CHANGE_IND is NULL
   AND SPRIDEN_PIDM IN (SELECT SARADAP_PIDM
                          FROM SARADAP
                         WHERE SARADAP_TERM_CODE_ENTRY = :TermCode
                           AND SARADAP_PIDM in (SELECT SARAPPD_PIDM
                                                  FROM SARAPPD
                                                 WHERE SARAPPD_APDC_CODE in ('P','N')
                                                   AND SARAPPD_TERM_CODE_ENTRY = SARADAP_TERM_CODE_ENTRY)
                           AND SARADAP_PIDM not in (SELECT SARAPPD_PIDM
                                                      FROM SARAPPD
                                                     WHERE SARAPPD_APDC_CODE in('C','D')
                                                       AND SARAPPD_TERM_CODE_ENTRY = SARADAP_TERM_CODE_ENTRY))
   AND SPRIDEN_PIDM not in (SELECT SFRSTCR_PIDM
                              FROM SFRSTCR
                             WHERE SFRSTCR_TERM_CODE = :TermCode
                               AND SFRSTCR_RSTS_CODE in ('RE','RW','AU'))

The code for applicants is mostly the same, but we have a "Paid Deposit" and "Non Paid Deposit" (the non-paid deposit is used for special circumstances, like high school students taking a class, where we don't collect our deposit fee)  decision which indicates that a student has committed - we don't allow admitted students without a deposit to register here.

 

The Bottom piece uses the registration table and has the Registration statuses for "registered", "Web Registered" and "Audit," since its possible a student went in and signed up for a class, then dropped it without adding more - they'd still have the SFRSTCR record with the dropped code, but no actively registered codes.  There may be a way for you to use a subquery on STVRSTS to determine enrolled vs. non-enrolled, but we've gone with enumeration due to ease of writing/reading.  ALSO - our registrars office is good about running changes by us prior to updating validation tables, so we don't have to worry about students "Disappearing" from any reports.
