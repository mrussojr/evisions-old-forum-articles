I've done some heavy digging and so far have found out the following:

 

In Banner, to get the admitting campus, we match SORLCUR against SARADAP and when there is a match, that is our admitting campus.

 

To get the current campus, we match SORLCUR against SGBSTDN and when there is a match, that is our current campus.  If there is no match, then the admitting campus is considered to be the current campus.

Hi David,

 

Yes, we have multiple campuses.  Here are the rules we use to determine the COGA (Campus of Greatest Attendance) looking only at credit enrollments:

  • If the student is not enrolled, use the campus of record (SORLCUR)
  • If the student enrolled in more courses at one campus than other campus, use that campus
  • If there is a tie in the greatest number of courses at multiple locations, use the campus of record if that is one of the tied campuses.  Otherwise, pick the first campus alphabetically.

The query looks at SFRSTCR, STVRSTS, and SSBSECT to determine the enrollments of the most recent term.

You may want to check with someone in the registrar's office to be sure of how to find the current campus. For us the the MIN SGBSTDN would have the campus of admission and the MAX <= current term would have the current campus.
