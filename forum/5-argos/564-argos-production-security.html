Hi,

 

We do not use the web viewer (due to security and aesthetic concerns).  As such, everyone has to use the Argos client.  Most of our users cannot make changes in production folders (even if they are datablock designers).  Only a handful of authorized users can move from development and testing folders to the production folders.  This mitigates most inadvertent changes.  HTH

Hi Todd,

 

Our security concerns with the Web Viewer were raised 2-3 years ago (perhaps more).  IIRC, authenticated users we able to run reports to which they did not have access.  Understandably, this was a huge concern for us and for Evisions.  I think Evisions has fixed the issues sind then.  But since we decided not to use the Web Viewer, we did not do a thorough retesting of the issue.  For our user community, the aesthetic issues were a bigger concern.  No one has asked in the last two years about the Web Viewer (the people who wanted to implement are no longer at the College).  So, this has been a back burner issue for us.  

 

We had plans to show web reports to students and other users that do not have access to Argos.  But, as we have been rolling out Banner 9, this has been less of an issue.  Also, my team writes custom portlets to fill in the gaps.

Hi David,

 

In our instance,  for our production folders the DataBlock Designers do not have Modify or Create permissions.  This means they can only run items in the production folders (assuming they have access).  In our development folders, however, DB Designers do have Modify & Create.  This means they can make changes there.  We have a separate role for users that can move datablocks from our development and testing folders into our production folders.
